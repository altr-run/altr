Proportions

Geometry

Built on a circle of radius r. The stem rises r; the fork opens on vectors (−r, −2r) and (+r, −2r) — an angle of 63.4° from stem to each branch. Stroke width is r ÷ 5.

Two strokes, one choice

The ochre branch signals the alternative. It can swap to walnut, moss, or ink depending on context — but only one branch ever carries the accent.

Proportions

The A as doorway

Apex at ¼ width, slightly left of center; crossbar at ⅗ height. The inner stroke (ochre) follows the left diagonal down, then walks inward along the crossbar — a hinge swinging open.

Why it works

The opening is the trademark. It's unmistakable at display size, and dissolves cleanly into a pure A at 16 px — where the inner stroke thickens into a solid counter.
