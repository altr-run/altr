§ Manifesto · from the founders

Product work
is fragmented.

Every product team we know runs the same broken relay: Linear holds the tickets, Notion holds the specs, Slack holds the decisions, GitHub holds the truth. And in 2026, a dozen AI agents sit in each tool, each with its own context, each forgetting what happened in the others.

You end up paying for four workspaces to get one loop done. The loop leaks at every handoff. Context is the thing that gets lost — and context is the only thing that matters when you're trying to ship.

"The unit of work isn't the ticket.
It's the loop."

Altr is one workspace for the whole loop. Specs, chat, code, review, changelog — all in one window, all sharing one graph. Humans drive. AI teammates — Pax writes specs, Eng writes code — work inside the same surface, seeing everything you see, answering to the same acceptance criteria.

We've made it local-first because your roadmap is nobody else's business. We've made it Mac-native because your tools should feel like your tools. And we've made it keyboard-first because the fastest operators we know never touch the mouse.

If you've ever felt the friction between "I know what we need to build" and "the repo reflects that" — we built Altr for you.

A — Mukul & the Altr team · San Francisco · April 2026
+ What you gain

One loop.
One workspace.

The shared graph behind every Altr surface — so every teammate, human or AI, works from the same source of truth.

AI teammates with standing context

Pax and Eng see every spec, PR, and decision — same as a human colleague who's been on the team for a year.

Specs that flow into PRs automatically

Click "build" on a spec. Eng spawns a worktree, implements, tests, opens the PR. You review; Altr merges.

A command bar that drives the whole loop

⌘K routes you — and the agents — across inbox, specs, PRs, channels, changelog. Never reach for the mouse.

Ship log that writes itself

Pax drafts the changelog from the week's merged PRs. You tweak it, click publish. Web + email + Slack.

= What you keep

Your stack.
Your rules.

Altr sits on top of what you already trust — GitHub is still source of truth, Slack is still where the team gossips, Figma is still the file.

GitHub, unchanged

Every worktree is a real branch. Every PR is a real PR. Your CI, your reviewers, your merge queue — all still there.

Slack, Linear, Notion — still yours

Altr mirrors threads both ways. Your team can stay in the tools they like; Altr is where the loop closes.

Your API keys, your model choice

BYO Anthropic or OpenAI. Or use our credits. Either way: training opt-out enforced. Nothing becomes training data.

Human in the driver's seat

Budget caps per worktree. Human approval required before merge. Agents suggest; you decide.

§ 06 · Questions

Straight
answers.

If yours isn't here, email hello@altr.run — a human reads every one.

Is Altr Mac-only?+
For now, yes. Mac-native, not Electron. Windows and Linux are on the 2027 roadmap. We'd rather ship one great client than three mediocre ones.
Where do my files live?+
On your Mac, encrypted at rest. Agents call models over your API keys; nothing becomes training data. Full details in the security whitepaper.
Which models power the teammates?+
Pax runs on Claude Sonnet. Eng wraps Claude Code. BYO keys, or use ours with $20 of credits per seat per month on Studio.
How is this different from Devin?+
Devin is an autonomous cloud engineer. Altr is a workspace where humans and several agents collaborate on your machine, with humans in the driver's seat. Different problem, different shape.
When is public launch?+
Private beta June 2026, public launch Q4 2026. Request access to join the beta cohort.
Do you have SOC 2?+
Type II audit in progress; report expected Q3 2026. Type I and DPA available today for Studio and Scale customers.
Can I self-host?+
Scale customers can run Altr on a dedicated instance, on-prem, or in a VPC of their choice. Talk to sales for specifics.
