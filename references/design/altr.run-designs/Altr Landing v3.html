Every spec is multiplayer.

Pax drafts acceptance criteria in your voice, holds the open questions, and tracks every edit back to who made it — human or agent. No more "the doc says X, Slack says Y."

  • Voice & style learning — Pax picks up your glossary, tone, and taste across specs.

Feature 02 · Autopilot engineering

Eng ships real PRs, not snippets.

Eng spawns Claude Code in an isolated git worktree, reads your repo conventions, implements the spec, runs your test suite, and opens a draft PR — so review stays in GitHub, not in a chat log.

  • Isolated worktrees — every job runs sandboxed. No stomping on your branch.
  • Policy gates — require human approval before merging, or auto-merge on green CI.
  • Budget controls — token + time caps per job. Hard-stop on overrun.

§ 05 · Security

Your files.
Your machine. Your rules.

Altr is local-first by design. Specs, transcripts, and repos live on your Mac, encrypted. Agents call out to models over your keys — nothing enters anyone's training data.

Local-first storage

Specs, conversations, and worktrees persist on-device in an encrypted store. You hold the keys — literally, in your keychain.

AES-256 at rest

Zero-training guarantee

Agents use your own Anthropic / OpenAI keys with training opt-out enforced. Your content cannot enter any model's training set.

BYO keys · no-training

Full audit trail

Every agent action — read, write, merge, send — is logged with attribution, exportable as JSON, SIEM, or signed PDF.

Immutable · exportable

Policy controls

Set per-agent budgets, require human approval for merges, restrict tool access by channel. Enforced in-process, not at the UI.

Budget · approval · scope

SSO & SCIM

Okta, Google Workspace, Azure AD. SCIM provisioning for team plans. Short-lived device tokens — no shared passwords.

SAML · SCIM 2.0

SOC 2 & privacy

§ 09 · Questions

Straight
answers.

If yours isn't here, email hello@altr.run — a human reads every one.

Is Altr Mac-only? +
For now, yes. Mac-native, not Electron. Windows and Linux are on the 2027 roadmap. We'd rather ship one great client than three mediocre ones.
Where do my files live? +
On your Mac, encrypted at rest. Agents call models over your API keys; nothing becomes training data. Full details in the security whitepaper.
Which models power the teammates? +
Pax runs on Claude Sonnet. Eng wraps Claude Code. You can BYO Anthropic/OpenAI keys, or use ours with $20 of credits per seat per month on Studio.
Why not a plugin for Linear or Cursor? +
Because the unit of work is the loop, not the ticket or the file. Plugins leave you tab-switching. Altr puts spec, chat, and code in one window with shared context — which is structurally something a plugin can't do.
How is this different from Devin? +
Devin is an autonomous cloud engineer. Altr is a workspace where humans and several agents collaborate on your machine, with humans in the driver's seat. Different problem, different shape.
When is public launch? +
Private beta June 2026, public launch Q4 2026. Request access today to join the beta cohort.
Do you have SOC 2? +
Type II audit in progress; report expected Q3 2026. Type I and DPA available today for Studio and Scale customers.
Can I self-host? +
Scale (Enterprise) customers can run Altr on a dedicated instance, on-prem, or in a VPC of their choice. Talk to sales for specifics.
