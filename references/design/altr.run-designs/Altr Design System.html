01 — Pillars

Brand pillars

Three statements. Every decision — product, visual, copy — should feel like it could only have come from a place that believes these.

I

Altr is a room, not a robot — a place you and your AI teammates sit down to do real work.

II

Altr is craft at new scale — the care of a small studio, the reach of a full team.

III

Altr is quiet on purpose — confidence without noise, intelligence without theatre.

06 — Logo

Wordmark exploration

Five directions. Each is a single idea stated clearly. Originals — no borrowed marks. My conviction lies with (C) Wordmark + Threshold glyph and (D) The leaning A.

A · SERIF

Old-world serif wordmark

A confident Instrument Serif / Sectra-weight wordmark with a discreet lt ligature — a nod to the craft roots, to type set in metal, to something made by hand. Reads as a small press, not a startup.

The leaning A — one idea, all type

The 'A' loses its crossbar and tilts its right leg slightly inward, so the apex sits off-center. Subtle enough to be a signature, not a stunt. Suggests 'altered.' The rest of the word is left perfectly still.

E · WILDCARD

The margin mark

Altr is set in serif, lowercase, perfectly plain — but preceded by a small hand-drawn ochre bracket, as if a human editor marked the word in the margin of a manuscript. The brand mark is the annotation, not the word: the idea that Altr is what happens when a person alters a plan. It refuses to behave like a tech logo at all.
