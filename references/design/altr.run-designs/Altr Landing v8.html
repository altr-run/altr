§ 01 · a note from the team

A short manifesto, because we kept having the same meeting.

“

Software teams keep inventing new ways to coordinate, and somehow we keep inventing the same new way. A Slack channel becomes a Notion doc becomes a Linear ticket becomes a branch becomes a PR becomes a Slack channel. The knowledge leaks at every boundary. Context dies at every handoff.

We think the thread should just be the work. The conversation where an idea is born should be the same surface where it turns into a spec, a branch, a draft PR, and a merged change. Not a digital paper trail — a single continuous document.

We also think AI coworkers deserve real roles, not magic wands. Not a chatbot that answers questions. Not a copilot that autocompletes. A coworker with a name, a memory, a role you can review — one you can disagree with in the margins and trust to catch what you missed.

Altr is what happens when you pull those two threads. It's the workspace where humans and AI ship together — as peers, in public, with the full audit trail — and where the next sprint starts where the last one left off. No more status updates. No more "where's the spec?". Just the work, and everyone, including the agents, pulling their weight.

Thank you for being early. We're building this in the open, with our beta teams, one sprint at a time.

”
Maya & Rav
Maya Chen & Rav Okafor

§ 03 · how it works

Five minutes from idea to draft PR.

01

Open a thread

Start the way you always do — a rough idea in a Slack-style thread. Mention @spec.

02

Structure the spec

@spec turns the thread into AC, open questions, and owner callouts. You edit; they follow.

03

Draft the PR

@eng opens a branch, plans the work, and drafts the diff — with tests, in the open.

04

Review & ship

You review like any teammate's PR. Comment, request changes, merge. The loop closes.

      § 12 · frequently asked

      A few things people ask.

      Is this another AI pair programmer?+
      No. Pair programmers live in your editor and help you write code faster. Altr lives where the team lives — the thread, the spec, the PR — and treats AI as coworkers, not autocomplete. You can absolutely use a pair programmer and Altr. They don't overlap.
      What happens to our existing tools?+
      Most teams keep their IDE, their CI, and their repo. They replace the coordination layer: Slack threads, Notion specs, Linear tickets. Altr integrates with all of them if you'd rather migrate piece by piece.
      Who owns the code the agents write?+
      You do. Every line is a commit with a co-author trailer naming the agent and the human reviewer. Copyright is yours. No training. No derivative claims.
      What models do the agents use?+
      Each role picks the best current model for the job — typically a frontier reasoning model for @spec and @review, a fast code model for @eng. On Enterprise you can bring your own keys or run models on-prem.
      Can we use Altr on private repos?+
      Yes. Altr runs agents in per-tenant sandboxes; your code never leaves your tenant without a signed request. SOC 2 Type II, HIPAA-ready, on-prem available.
      When does GA ship?+
      We're in closed beta with 140 teams right now (Apr 2026). GA is penciled for Q3 2026. Beta seats are free; pricing above applies at GA.
